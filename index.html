Time to Hack Urban Ag!

This Spring and Summer, MashambaHack is inviting urban farmers, hackers, designers, & entrepreneurs to innovate Atlanta's urban food web with open source technology to position ATL as a global leader in urban ag tech!

It is estimated that 70% of humans will live in cities by the year 2050! This growing pressure to feed global urban populations will only increase and although this problem is greatest felt in developing nations, our own city has numerous food deserts in disadantaged communities.

However! It's Atlanta's unique qualities of having a strong urban ag movement, startup ecology, and technology community that can contribute to us becoming global leaders in urban food production!

MashambaHack, with our partners, is facilitating a three-phase urban food web innovation event that will organize an urban ag tech working group with the mission of creating at least eight new projects that will directly benefit urban farmer in our city and beyond:

Phase 1:
The Seed

We start MashambaHack with an UrbanAgTech Hackathon!

Phase 2A+B:
Germination+Bloom

We continue working several weeks using meetups, workshops, and code days.

Phase 3:
The Harvest

We culminate MashambaHack with Demo Day!

Event Timeline

Hackathons are normally about moving fast-fast-fast, but food is slow. Good food is slower!

Systems architecture simply takes time. It requires a deliberate, intentional approach to bring stake holders together, frame the problems, get out the building (ie, visit farms, production sites), and continuously iterate solutions based on customer feedback to disrupt these long held patterns that are no longer viable.

To do so, MashambaHack uses the following format to foster innovation in local food systems:

  • Early Spring 2016

    The Hackathon: "SEED"

    We initiate MashambaHack with a 48-hour UrbanAgTech hackathon; creating a working group of farmers, hackers, and designers organized into several cross-functional teams capable of targeting one or more challenges. Cooperation, especially between teams, is emphasized over competition.

  • Late Spring 2016

    Deep Diving: "GERMINATION"

    The hacking continues! Teams have formed and they've begun to deep dive into their target problems. They will get out the building to visit urban farmers and other urban food web projects over the next several weeks. MashambaHack meetups will occur at least monthly for checkups, workshops, and intra-team collaboration. Speakers, mentors, and tech evangelists will be scheduled to provide additional insight and expertise.

  • Late Summer 2016

    Refining Solutions: "BLOOM"

    At this point, MashambaHack teams have had weeks to "get out the building" to talk with actual urban farmers and their customers during the peak of the growing season. Now they need to begin polishing their solutions in preperation for Demo Day presentations. We will provided ample opportunity to for them to meet, hear from bus dev experts and mentors, as well as get technical support from evangelists.

  • Early Fall 2016

    Demo Day: "HARVEST"!

    It's time for our teams to present their solutions to the community! MashambaHack teams get to demonstrate their solutions to a panel of judges representative of the agricultural, civic, tech and business community. Currently, prizes are TBA and open to suggestions from our sponsors!

We can't rush food and expect phenomenal results. Ultimately, the opportunity is huge for a city like Atlanta with a deep agricultural history and cutting edge tech community to cement our role as a global business leader in urban ag tech! We can raise global metropolitan resiliency, create more economic opportunities here at home, diversify our own food web, and increase the level of wellness of our city!
